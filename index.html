Introduction

Villa Foscari is a Patrician Villa in Mira, near Venice, northern Italy, designed by the Italian architect Andrea Palladio.

It is also known as La Malcontenta, a nickname which it received when the spouse of one of the Foscaris was locked up in the house because she allegedly didn't live up to her conjugal duty.

More informations and tips can be found at: 1. Wikipedia 2. Main-Page

Hierarchical decomposition of the model

During to approach to the creation of three-dimensional model, we chose to hierarchically decompose the structure of the villa into five-plus-one parts:

  • The Twin Stairs and the Colonnade-Basement
  • The Main Building
  • The Colonnade (better, A column)
  • The Main Roof and the Roof upon the Colonnade
  • The Back-Side of the Villa
  • All the other stuff (such as windows, banisters, gratings...)

Twin Stairs and Colonnade-Basement

Due its symmetrical nature, Villa Foscari can be split in a right-half and a left-half.

Let's concentrate on the last one, because we'll obtain the other half simply with a S trasformation. Moreover Rotational trasformations, R, are used to create the upper side of the stairs and due its squareness, we'll use mostly CUBOID functions.

The same trick can be used to draw the Colonnade_Basement, which is exactly between the twin stairs.

Main Building

The Colonnade

The colonnade is the most complex part of the project as it was decided to model the capital as an Archimedean spiral drawn by a NUBS (Archimedean spiral). It was also used a light-domain to avoid overloading the global model.

To draw the column instead, we chose to adopt divide-and-conquer-approach:

  • Column-Base with NUBS and ROTATIONAL SURFACE.
  • Column-Shaft with a CYLINDER_SURFACE
  • Column-Capital still with NUBS ROTATIONAL SURFACE and BEZIER

The entire Colonnade, which is basically a column replicated and then translated through the function REPLICA, counts ten columns.

The Main Roof and the Roof upon the Colonnade

The roof upon the colonnade has been modeled with CUBOID roto-traslated. The Main Roof counts a SCALE colonnade-roof, combined with 2*CUBOID and 2*TRIANGLE_DOMAIN advisably rotated and translated.

The Back_Side of the Villa

With its circular glass, along with its asymmetrical facade, the back-side of the villa is one of the most complex part of the model. The glass has been modeled by a DISK, surrounded by CUBOID on all its perimeter. The glass just above the circular one, has been modeled by a 2D DOMAIN and a TRIANGLE_DOMAIN.

Last but not least: All the other stuff

At the end of our rendering process we did take care of details, such as:

  • Gratings

  • Banisters

  • Windows and Shutters

  • Chimneys-pot upon the Main roof
