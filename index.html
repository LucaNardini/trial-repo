Introduction

Villa Foscari is a Patrician Villa in Mira, near Venice, northern Italy, designed by the Italian architect Andrea Palladio.
It is also known as La Malcontenta, a nickname which it received when the spouse of one of the Foscaris was locked up in the house because she allegedly didn't live up to her conjugal duty.

More informations and tips can be found at: 1. Wikipedia 2. Main-Page

Hierarchical decomposition of the model

During to approach to the creation of three-dimensional model, we chose to hierarchically decompose the structure of the villa into five-plus-one contexts:

  • The Basement: The Twin Stairs, the Colonnade-Basement and the Garden
  • The Main Building
  • The Colonnade (precisely, A column)
  • The Front Facade of the Villa: The Main Roof and the Tympanum
  • The Back-Side of the Villa: Circular Glass and Back Facade
  • All the other stuff: windows, banisters, gratings and chimneyspot

Please note: the code has been implemented with export functions, which means each of such contexts it's independent from others and can be drawn singularly.

Twin Stairs and Colonnade-Basement

Due its symmetrical nature, Villa Foscari can be split in a right-half and a left-half.

If we focus on the last one, we'll obtain the other half simply with a S trasformation. Moreover, Rotational trasformations, R, are used to create the upper side of the stairs and due its squareness, we'll use mostly CUBOID functions.

The same trick can be used to draw the Colonnade_Basement, which is exactly between the twin stairs.

Main Building

Please notice that every window, before being applied to the model, passes through a BOUNDARY function for aesthetic purpose.

The Colonnade

The colonnade is the most complex part of the project as it was decided to model the capital as an Archimedean spiral drawn by a NUBS (Archimedean spiral). It was also used a light-domain to avoid overloading the global model.

To draw the column instead, we chose to adopt divide-and-conquer-approach:

  • Column-Base with NUBS and ROTATIONAL SURFACE.
  • Column-Shaft with a CYLINDER_SURFACE
  • Column-Capital still with NUBS ROTATIONAL SURFACE and BEZIER

The entire Colonnade, which is basically a column replicated and then translated through the function REPLICA, counts ten columns advisably placed upon the Colonnade Basement.

The Main Roof and Tympanum

The tympanum has been modeled with CUBOID roto-traslated. The Main Roof counts a SCALE colonnade-roof, combined with 2*CUBOID and 2*TRIANGLE_DOMAIN advisably rotated and translated.

The Backside of the Villa

With its circular glass, along with its asymmetrical facade, the back-side of the villa is one of the most complex part of the model. The glass just above the circular one, has been modeled by a 2D DOMAIN and a TRIANGLE_DOMAIN.
